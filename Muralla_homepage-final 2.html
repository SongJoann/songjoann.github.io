ARTISTS IN RESIDENCE   

  • Albert Oehlen
    There’s something eternally youthful about the German painter, who first came to prominence in the 1980s and, along with his partners in crime Martin Kippenberger and Werner Büttner, redefined what painting could be. Earlier this month, TASCHEN published an epically sized Oehlen monograph featuring 400 works.               
  • Simone Leigh
    Last week, it was announced that Simone Leigh would make history at the Venice Biennale by representing the United States in 2022, making her the first Black woman ever to do the country’s pavilion at the world’s top art festival.
  • Daniel Arsham

    What drives an artist, inspired by principles of timelessness and craftsmanship, to enter an industry typified by novelty and throwaway excess? The answer - like so much of Daniel Arsham’s work - lies within the paradox.

     

     

  • Takashi Murakami
    Takashi Murakami is awash in a blizzard of beaming, multicolored flowers. He’s walking along an outdoor corridor under the Broad museum’s architectural shell and the manic-looking flowers swarm through the air in his direction. They hover above his head, blinking at the artist..
  • Luo Zhongli

    Luo Zhongli is one of China’s leading realistic modern artists. As opposed to other artists’ abstract paintings of faces, his powerful photorealistic portraits of people in rural southwestern China have deeply moved people all over the world; it is as though they open up a window into the lives and souls of these people.

  • Sean Scully
    Irish artist Sean Scully often works with stripes, grids, and dark tones to create his characteristic abstract works. There is always a dialogue between geometry and soft edges, something that Scully himself describes as the “battle between system and emotion. Scully carries a fascinating collection of cultural influences with him.
